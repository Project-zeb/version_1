ABOUT DISASTER

Disasters may arise from natural hazards—such as earthquakes, floods, cyclones, and landslides—or from human-induced events, including industrial accidents, fires, and environmental degradation. They often occur suddenly and can have long-lasting impacts on infrastructure, livelihoods, and public health. Effective disaster risk management involves preparedness, mitigation, response, and recovery strategies aimed at minimizing losses and enhancing the resilience of communities.
